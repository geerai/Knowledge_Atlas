Propose an Article

Add a candidate paper to Knowledge Atlas, check whether it fits scope, and send it into review with enough context for a fast decision.
Step 1 — Identify the Article
Enter the DOI to automatically fetch the abstract and citation details from CrossRef. If the DOI is unknown, fill in the APA citation manually.
If the article is already in KA, the system will tell you immediately — no duplicate submissions needed. If it is not, you will be guided through the relevance check.
CrossRef resolves most DOIs to full citation metadata. If your DOI fails, enter the citation manually below.
Use APA 7th edition. Include DOI as a URL at the end if available.
Step 2 — Relevance Decision
Read the abstract above (or below) and decide whether this article is relevant to KA's scope: environmental factors affecting cognitive performance, wellbeing, or physiological state in workplace or learning environments. Peer-reviewed, empirical, published 1990–present.
KA inclusion criteria: peer-reviewed · empirical (has data) · relevant to at least one KA concept node (attention, fatigue, stress, lighting, thermal comfort, noise, crowding, biophilic design, wayfinding, or spatial cognition) · published 1990 or later · not a review article (unless it is a meta-analysis with pooled effect sizes).

? Uncertain — marginal
✕ Exclude — not relevant
Step 3 — Upload PDF (optional but strongly encouraged)
Upload the PDF only if you have legitimate access to it through UCSD library, open access, or direct author copy. Do not upload pirated PDFs. The system verifies that the file is a genuine PDF before accepting it.
PDF acquisition methods (in order of reliability): (1) Elicit direct library link — fastest; (2) Unpaywall / open access version; (3) Google Scholar "All versions"; (4) Zotero browser connector; (5) Email the author. UCSD library access covers the majority of journals in this domain.

Step 4 — Submit for Review
Your submission will be reviewed by a KA maintainer within 48 hours. If approved and the PDF is present, the article enters the extraction queue for claim parsing. Citation-level records (no PDF) still count toward the article target.
