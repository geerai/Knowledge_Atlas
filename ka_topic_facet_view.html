How to read this page

The Atlas crosswalk links human outcomes, architectural conditions, and defended topic bundles. Use it when the ordinary taxonomy is too coarse and you want to ask a joined question such as lighting and stress or nature and attention.

Data source

This page reads data/ka_payloads/topic_crosswalk.json. If the richer renderer fails, the page falls back to a plain defended-bundle list rather than leaving you with a broken screen.

How this page works

The Knowledge Atlas crosswalk is a bridge between three things: (a) human outcomes people care about (stress, attention, wellbeing, sleep…), (b) architectural conditions and stimulus families (luminous, acoustic, spatial, natural…), and (c) defended topic bundles that link papers, theories, and measures. This page lets you travel across the bridge.

What each step does

  • Stage 0 — entry. Pick whether you want to start with an outcome, an architectural feature, or see the full landscape at once.
  • Stage 1 — first facet. Outcomes are grouped into categories (Cognitive, Affective, Physiological & Health, Behavioural, Other). Click a category header to expand its options.
  • Stage 2 — second facet. Only the architectural families (or outcomes) with defended bundles for your first choice are shown. You can also skip this step and see all bundles for your first choice.
  • Stage 3 — results. A list of defended topic bundles, each with paper count and evidence-status pill (defended / mixed / unresolved).

Breadcrumb & reset

A breadcrumb at the top of each stage shows what you've picked so far (e.g. OUTCOME: stress › ARCHITECTURE: luminous). Click start over to return to Stage 0.

When to use the other variants

  • Heatmap View — when you want to see coverage density across the whole 9 × 18 matrix at a glance.
  • Full Facets — when you already know the vocabulary and want every chip visible at once.
  • Dashboard View — when you want three linked panels updating in lockstep as you browse.
  • Quick Lookup — when you want a single one-way answer (pick outcome, see families).

Evidence-status pills

  • defended — the topic bundle has a defended paper set and a resolved outcome mapping. Highest confidence.
  • mixed — evidence set has known quality issues; use with caution.
  • unresolved — the outcome term could not be resolved to the Outcome Contractor canonical vocabulary. Present for transparency; treat as provisional.
