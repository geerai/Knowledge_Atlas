## 1. What this deliverable is

The Knowledge Atlas ships five distinct topic-page variants — the same underlying crosswalk data (102 defended topic bundles × 18 outcomes × 9 architectural families) rendered in five different interaction patterns (`ka_topic_facet_view.html`, `ka_topic_heatmap_view.html`, `ka_topic_full_facets_view.html`, `ka_topic_dashboard_view.html`, `ka_topic_quick_lookup_view.html`). The instructional question is *which variant works best for whom?* This deliverable asks the student, working in the UX track, to put each variant through the three user-archetype scenarios they built in T4.b and produce a comparative evaluation with a concrete design recommendation.

The deliverable is deliberately comparative because the five variants are not substitutes: each is optimised for a different cognitive task. A heatmap is fastest for auditors checking coverage density; a progressive-disclosure flow is best for first-time visitors who do not know the vocabulary; a full-facet filter is most powerful for users who know what they want. The student's job is to render those trade-offs legible by walking every archetype through every variant and reporting where the variant serves the archetype and where it doesn't.

## 2. What the student must produce

A **single evaluation package** at `160sp/tracks/t4/{student_id}/T4.b.2/` containing:

1. `scenarios_referenced.md` — a one-page recap (or a symlink) to the three user archetypes and their scenarios from T4.b. For completeness; so a reviewer can read this deliverable end-to-end without hunting across folders.

2. `walkthrough_matrix.csv` — a 15-row table (3 archetypes × 5 variants), each row containing:
   - `archetype`  (160_student / public_visitor / admin)
   - `scenario_id` (e.g. `160_student_find_track_evidence`)
   - `variant_id` (facet / heatmap / full_facets / dashboard / quick_lookup)
   - `task_completion` (succeeded / partial / failed)
   - `clicks_to_success` (integer, 0 if failed)
   - `time_to_success_sec` (student's measured time in that walkthrough)
   - `first_friction_point` (short free text)
   - `recoverable_without_help` (yes / no)
   - `notes` (one or two sentences)

3. `findings/` — **five to seven structured findings files** following the T4 finding schema (from `T4.a_heuristic_audit.md` § "Finding-file schema"). Each file names one specific friction point or affordance, attaches one screenshot, and rates severity on Nielsen's 1–5 scale. Findings must cite the `walkthrough_matrix.csv` rows they are derived from.

4. `comparison_report.md` (~ 900–1,400 words). Structure:
   - **§1 Scenarios summary** (~ 150 words): the three archetypes and what they were trying to do.
   - **§2 Per-archetype variant preference** (~ 250 words): for each archetype, which variant(s) worked best, and why. Cite matrix rows.
   - **§3 Cross-cutting pattern observations** (~ 250 words): failures shared by multiple variants, successes that only one variant enabled, structural trade-offs.
   - **§4 Per-variant strengths and weaknesses** (~ 250 words): one paragraph per variant with at least two specific behaviours observed.
   - **§5 Design recommendation** (~ 250 words): which variant should be the default landing at `ka_topics.html`, and which should be reachable one click away. If the student proposes a hybrid, describe its specific affordances.
   - **§6 Reflection** (~ 250 words): what the student learned about the bridge structure by watching users travel across it; what they would measure next if given another week.

5. `reflection.md` — standard ~ 300-word reflection (separate from §6 above; the report's §6 is evaluation-focused, this one is about the student's own learning).

## 4. Scoring bands (three criteria, 0–3, rescaled to 10 points)

### Completeness

- **0** — Matrix missing, or fewer than 10 matrix rows, or fewer than 3 findings, or comparison report < 500 words.
- **1** — Matrix has 10–14 rows, findings 3–4, comparison report 500–849 words.
- **2** — All 15 matrix rows, 5+ findings with schema, comparison report ≥ 850 words and all six sections present.
- **3** — All band-2 plus 7+ findings, comparison report ≥ 1,100 words, reflection ≥ 320 words.

### Quality

- **0** — Matrix rows are fabricated (task-completion flags don't match the observed behaviour described in notes), findings don't cite matrix rows, design recommendation is generic.
- **1** — Walkthroughs are real but shallow (all clicks_to_success are identical round numbers; notes are single-sentence placeholders); findings adequate but don't reference specific variant behaviours; design recommendation proposes Pattern 2 or Pattern 5 with no engagement with trade-offs.
- **2** — Walkthroughs are differentiated across archetypes; findings cite specific variant behaviours; design recommendation engages with the per-archetype preference from §2 of the report.
- **3** — Walkthroughs show clear per-archetype and per-variant distinctions (e.g., public visitors routinely stall on the Full Facets variant at a specific chip, but navigate the Facet View without help); the recommendation names both a specific default variant and a specific escape-hatch variant and justifies why the combination covers the observed archetype preferences.

### Reflection

Standard reflection bands from `rubrics/README.md § 3`. Consistency check enforced: every variant-id in the reflection must also appear in the walkthrough matrix; every friction point in the reflection must also appear in a finding file.

## 5. How to connect T4.b.2 to T4.b (the instruction set the student follows)

### 5.1 Read your three T4.b archetypes

Open `160sp/tracks/t4/{your_id}/findings/T4.b_{role}.md` for each of the three roles you wrote in T4.b:

- `160_student` — an enrolled COGS 160 student trying to find evidence for their track work.
- `public_visitor` — a researcher or practitioner unfamiliar with the Atlas, landing on the topic page from a Google search.
- `admin` — the instructor (DK or a TA) auditing coverage and grade state.

Re-read the three scenarios you wrote per archetype. They should already be concrete: "Aisha (160 student, Track 1) wants to find the five highest-paper-count topic bundles involving `luminous` to prioritise her image-tagging schema" is concrete; "a student wants to learn about lighting" is not — if your T4.b scenarios are the latter, sharpen them first before starting T4.b.2.

### 5.2 Walk every archetype through every variant

For each of the 3 archetypes × 5 variants = 15 cells, open the variant page in a fresh browser tab, execute the archetype's primary scenario, and record in `walkthrough_matrix.csv`:

| What you're looking for | How to record it |
|---|---|
| Did the user succeed at the scenario's primary task? | `task_completion` column |
| How many clicks? | `clicks_to_success` |
| How long? | `time_to_success_sec` (measured with a stopwatch while you walked through) |
| What was the first moment of confusion or backtracking? | `first_friction_point` |
| Could the user recover without reading the Help modal or asking someone? | `recoverable_without_help` |
| One or two sentences on anything else worth noting | `notes` |

Do this for real. If you simulate the walkthrough in your head without actually executing it, the times and click-counts will be suspiciously uniform across the matrix and you will score band 1 on Quality.

### 5.3 Identify 5–7 findings

After the matrix is full, scan it for patterns. The finding types most worth writing up:

- **Per-archetype preference signals** — archetype X consistently succeeded faster on variant Y.
- **Variant-specific failures** — variant Z broke down for ≥ 2 of 3 archetypes at the same step.
- **Vocabulary mismatches** — the chip labels on Full Facets are opaque to the public_visitor; the Facet View's grouped accordion solves it. This is a finding, not an assumption.
- **Mobile-or-screen-size constraints** — Dashboard View has three columns that cramp below 820 px; note if any of your archetypes are likely mobile users.
- **Help-button reliance** — if the 160_student succeeds on Facet View only by reading the Help modal, that's a finding about the Help button's necessity.

Each finding gets its own `.md` file with the T4-standard schema (heuristic, scenario, observed, expected, severity, screenshot, recommendation).

### 5.4 Write the comparison report

The report is not a summary of the matrix. It is an analysis that pulls out per-archetype preferences (§2), shared patterns (§3), per-variant trade-offs (§4), and lands on a defended design recommendation (§5). The recommendation must name both a default variant AND an escape-hatch variant; if you propose a hybrid, specify what components of which variants the hybrid borrows.

### 5.5 Reflection

Standard T4 reflection: what did you learn about the bridge structure? What would you measure next given another week? What did walking five variants teach you that walking one wouldn't have?
