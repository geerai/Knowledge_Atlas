Choose Your Track

Four tracks, four capability gaps, one shared system. Each track produces real components that integrate into Knowledge Atlas. Browse the details below, then sign up.

How Tracks Work

Weeks 4–7 are track-specific sprints. Each track has its own syllabus, deliverables, and support. You work in teams of three to four students. Each sprint produces a real, tested component that connects to the Knowledge Atlas infrastructure. Your methodology log documents the process: the decisions you made, the obstacles you overcame, and the evidence that justified your choices.

Choosing a track is a serious commitment, but the choice reflects your interests and strengths. You have had two weeks to explore. If you have not decided by Week 3, this page and the track assignment pages will help you choose. The track signup process is straightforward: select a track, identify your teammates or indicate that you are willing to be grouped, and wait for formal team assignment. You can change tracks until the end of Week 3.

Detailed Track Descriptions

Track 1: Image Tagger

What you'll build: A tool and workflow for annotating architectural photographs with evidence-backed psychological constructs. Your system will verify that each tag is supported by the K-ATLAS evidence base — no invention, only evidence-driven classification.

Week-by-week:
Week 4: Design annotation schema and tag hierarchy. Build prototype tool.
Week 5: Pilot annotation on 200 images. Refine schema based on results.
Week 6: Full annotation run on 1,000 images. Validation and quality control.
Week 7: Final deliverables, usability feedback, integration testing.
Full Assignment →

Track 2: Article Finder

What you'll build: A query engine and batch retrieval system that finds relevant peer-reviewed articles for K-ATLAS research questions. Your system will handle deduplication, verify schema compliance, and maintain database integrity under real-world search conditions.

Week-by-week:
Week 4: Design query protocol. Build API connectors to Google Scholar and PubMed.
Week 5: Implement deduplication and schema validation. Test on 50 example queries.
Week 6: Scale to 130 K-ATLAS questions. Handle edge cases and failure modes.
Week 7: Final retrieval of 150+ curated citations. Integration with main system.
Full Assignment →

Track 3: VR Production

What you'll build: Interactive 3D environments that visualize epistemological concepts and warrant structures. Your prototypes will run in Unreal Engine or A-Frame and include sensor logging so users can see how their exploration affects the evidence graph.

Week-by-week:
Week 4: Learn engine, design environment spec, create initial 3D models.
Week 5: Implement interactive warrant visualization and user navigation.
Week 6: Add sensor logging and state persistence. Test interaction paradigm.
Week 7: Polish, documentation, final demo, transition to fall continuity.
Full Assignment →

Track 4: GUI Evaluation

What you'll build: A web interface for researchers to explore the K-ATLAS evidence graph. Your interface will be tested against acceptance criteria you write, documented in a usability report, and accompanied by a spec wall showing the decision history.

Week-by-week:
Week 4: Design acceptance criteria and user personas. Create wireframes and interaction spec.
Week 5: Build prototype interface. Implement core search and navigation features.
Week 6: User testing with 10–12 researchers. Document findings and iterate.
Week 7: Final interface, usability report, spec wall, integration testing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I switch tracks after signup?
Yes, through the end of Week 3 (by Thursday of Week 3). After teams form and team contracts are finalized, switching is no longer possible because your teammates depend on you. If you are uncertain about your choice, use this week to explore thoroughly.
What if I have no coding experience?
Every track involves coding, but the specific skills vary. Track 1 (Image Tagger) and Track 2 (Article Finder) are Python-heavy. Track 3 (VR Production) focuses on 3D design and visual programming. Track 4 (GUI Evaluation) emphasizes frontend development and user testing. Choose based on which skill set interests you most and which gap you think is most important. Your teammates will likely have complementary strengths.
Do I need VR hardware for Track 3?
No. You will develop using Unreal Engine or A-Frame, both of which run on standard laptops and can be tested in desktop mode. VR headset support is a bonus feature for Week 8 and the fall continuation, not a requirement for this quarter's deliverables.
What's the time commitment?
Weeks 4–7 are intensive. Expect 12–15 hours per week during sprint weeks, with roughly 8 hours in class and 4–7 hours outside class for your track's work. The actual load depends on how efficiently your team coordinates. Teams that plan well and divide labor clearly tend to run lean. Teams that improvise spend more time debugging.
What if I want to work alone?
Tracks are team-based. The minimum team size is three students. If you do not have teammates in mind, indicate that on the signup form and you will be matched with other unmatched students from your chosen track.
