## Status

## Inventory principles

1. Start from pathway families the panels already treated as important.
2. Keep the families broad enough for student literature finding.
3. Distinguish:
- likely direct-measurement areas
- mostly inferred or bridge areas
4. Record classic anchors as a collection task when they are not yet assembled.

## Initial pathway families

### 1. Predictive Processing

Core idea:
- environmental structure shapes prediction, prediction error, and precision-weighting dynamics

Likely linked theories:
- ART
- SRT
- Prospect-Refuge
- Kaplan Preference
- Soundscape

Likely student literature targets:
- predictive coding in perception
- environmental predictability
- prediction error and novelty
- precision weighting in stress/anxiety/autism-related perception

Current evidence status:
- strong as a broad framework
- often too easily overextended in design discourse

Collection status:
- needs classic anchor paper set

## Article-finder assignment model

Assign students by pathway family, not just by generic topic.

For each pathway family, the student team should produce:
1. `classic anchor papers`
2. `recent reviews`
3. `direct-measurement papers`
4. `environment-design relevant papers`
5. `short note on what is directly measured vs inferred`
