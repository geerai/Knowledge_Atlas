Purpose: give article finders a concrete first assignment model for the neural-underpinnings literature

## Why this exists

Track 2 should not operate as:
- random paper hunting
- or generic topic search

It should help build the Atlas intentionally.

One of the highest-value ways to do that is to deepen the neural-underpinnings literature around pathway families the panels already proposed.

## Assignment structure

Each Track 2 student or pair is assigned one pathway family.

They begin with:
1. classic anchor papers
2. major reviews
3. direct-measurement papers
4. design/environment papers that use or bear on the pathway

Their job is not only to collect papers.
It is to clarify:
- what is directly measured
- what is inferred
- where the evidence is strong
- where the pathway story is still thin

## First six pathway families

Start with these:

1. `Predictive Processing`
2. `Salience Networks`
3. `Interoceptive / Constructionist Affect`
4. `Neuromodulatory Systems`
5. `Chronobiological Regulation`
6. `Multisensory Integration`

These are the highest-yield starting set for the neural-underpinnings layer.

## Per-pathway deliverables

For each assigned pathway family, produce:

1. `Classic anchor set`
- 3 to 8 papers
- older or foundational
- each with one sentence on why it is classic

2. `Recent reviews`
- 2 to 4 papers
- ideally reviews, syntheses, or authoritative overviews

3. `Direct-measurement set`
- 3 to 8 papers
- papers that directly measure relevant neural, physiological, or pathway-level variables

4. `Environment-relevant set`
- 3 to 8 papers
- papers that connect the pathway to architectural, environmental, spatial, sensory, or design-relevant questions

5. `Short pathway note`
- one paragraph explaining:
  - what the pathway is
  - what parts are directly measured
  - what parts are usually inferred

6. `Open questions`
- 3 to 5 bullets on what remains unclear

## Search strategy

### Step 1 — Start from the Atlas docs

Use:
- `/Users/davidusa/REPOS/Knowledge_Atlas/docs/INITIAL_NEURAL_PATHWAY_INVENTORY_2026-03-24.md`
- `/Users/davidusa/REPOS/Article_Eater_PostQuinean_v1_recovery/docs/EXAMPLE_CATALOG_2026-02-24.md`
- relevant panel reduction docs when named there

### Step 2 — Find classic anchors

Search for:
- well-cited foundational papers
- canonical review papers
- classic named experiments or models

### Step 3 — Expand outward

From each anchor:
- cited by
- recent review articles
- direct neural measurement papers
- environmental/design applications

### Step 4 — Separate direct evidence from inference

For every promising paper, ask:
1. what pathway is proposed?
2. what is directly measured?
3. what is only inferred?

If the paper only speculates about the mechanism, do not discard it, but label it correctly.

## Output format

For each paper, record:
- `paper_title`
- `year`
- `authors`
- `paper_type`
- `why_it_matters`
- `pathway_role`
  - `classic_anchor`
  - `recent_review`
  - `direct_measurement`
  - `environment_relevant`
- `directly_measured_vs_inferred`
- `notes`

## Stop conditions

Stop when you have:
- at least 3 strong classic anchors
- at least 2 strong reviews
- at least 3 direct-measurement papers
- at least 3 environment-relevant papers
- a usable pathway note

Do not keep collecting weakly related papers just to make the list longer.

## Quality standards

Good submission:
- papers are clearly relevant
- classic anchors are genuinely foundational
- direct measurement is distinguished from inference
- the pathway note is clear and disciplined

Weak submission:
- vague relevance
- no separation between measured and inferred claims
- no sense of what the anchor papers actually contributed
